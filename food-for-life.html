food for life

Come hungry.
Leave nourished.

After every class at Yoga in the Grove, a free vegetarian meal is served — prasadam prepared with love, offered without charge, and shared by everyone in the room. No ticket. No sign-up. Just food and good company.

what is prasadam

Food prepared as an offering

Prasadam is a Sanskrit word meaning "mercy" or "grace." In the Bhakti tradition, food is first offered to God with love and prayer before it is shared — transforming a simple meal into something more than nutrition.

The idea is ancient and practical: what you eat affects how you feel, think, and practice. Prasadam is prepared without meat, fish, or eggs — sattvic food, considered in the yogic tradition to be the most conducive to clarity, steadiness, and inner peace.

the tradition

Rooted in Food for Life Global

Our meals are inspired by Food for Life Global — the world's largest plant-based food relief organization, founded on the principle that "pure food equals pure consciousness." Since 1974, Food for Life has served over six billion free meals in more than 65 countries.

At Yoga in the Grove, we carry that spirit into our small corner of Coconut Grove — one meal at a time, one class at a time.

"Pure food equals pure consciousness."

what to expect

i.

Always Free

No charge. No donation box. No expectation. The meal is offered freely to every person who comes through the door.

ii.

Always Vegetarian

Every dish is plant-based — freshly prepared, deeply seasoned, and made with care. Expect rice, dal, sabji, and rotating seasonal dishes.

iii.

After Every Class

The meal follows every class and Kirtan evening at 3220 Virginia Street. Stay, sit, eat, and meet the people you just practiced with.

why it matters

The meal is part of the practice.

In most studios, class ends and people disperse. Here, the meal is where the community actually forms. It is where strangers become regulars, where a first-timer stops feeling like a visitor, where the practice extends beyond the mat.

We have seen people come for the yoga and stay for the food. We have seen people come for the food and discover the yoga. Either way, they are welcome — and so are you.

Come as you are. Eat with us. That's it.
